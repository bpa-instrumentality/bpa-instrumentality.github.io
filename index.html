The Violin

The violin is a classical string instrument which consists of 4 strings: G, D, A, and E. The violin is a great instrument to learn playing, and helps build a strong foundation in music. Violins range from about $15 to 50$ a month, depending on the violin's size and quality. They come in a range of sizes, but the most common are the 1/4, 1/2, 3/4 and full size violins.

Fun Fact: Violin bows are generally made of 150-200 hairs.

The Guitar

The guitar is a strings instrument that is used in music genres such as country, rock, metal, and even jazz music. The guitar is characterized by its body which is covered in 6 strings. It is also characterized by the sound that it generates. The guitar's sound is very deep, with some bass in it. Guitars will cost you between $30-50 dollars based on the size of the guitar you are renting.

Fun Fact: The first guitar was probably made in ancient Greece around 3,000 years ago.

The Drums

The drums are a percussion instrument that uses multiple halo parts being hit to make a sound. There are 3 main types of drums: bass, snare, and tom. These drums can come in many sizes from small, medium to large. The drums's pricing is very versatile, and depends from instrument to instrument. On average, they are $8 - $32 per month.

The Saxophone

The saxophone is a brass musical instrument played by blowing air into a mouthpiece with a single reed. It has a conical shape with a narrow bell and a wider mouthpiece. There are several different types of saxophones, including soprano, alto, tenor, baritone, and bass. It is often used in jazz, blues, and popular music and is known for its warm, rich tone and versatility. The monthly price of saxophones are around $9-$21

The Trumpet

The trumpet is a brass musical instrument played by blowing air through a set of brass tubes. It has a flared end called the bell and a narrow end called the mouthpiece, where the player blows into the instrument. It is often used in classical, jazz, and popular music. Frequently Asked Questions

How do rentals/instrument rentals work?

+

Instrument rentals are a great way to have an instrument without going through the expenses of purchasing one. In general, to rent an instrument, you will agree to a rental period. Your instrument will be shipped to you as soon as possible after you make your first payment. You will pay a regular fee during the rental period to keep the instrument. You will return it to InstruMentality at the end of the rental period.

How do I order a rental?

+

Renting an instrument can be a convenient and cost-effective way to access the musical equipment you need. The first step to placing a rental order with our company is to identify the type of instrument you are interested in. This could be a guitar for a personal performance, a drum set for a school band, or any other type of instrument. Once you know what you need, you can place your order through Instrumentality's website. If you are unsure of what instrument is right for you, you can use Instrumentality's InstruFinder tool to help you find the perfect instrument. Once you find the right instrument, add it to your cart and check out. It's important to carefully read and understand the terms of the rental agreement before signing it, as this will outline the responsibilities and obligations of both parties. After you finish the purchase process, the instrument will be shipped to you as soon as possible.During your rental period, it's important to take good care of the instrument and return it on time to avoid any additional fees.

What do I do if my instrument needs repairs?

+

If your instrument needs repairs then go to our contact page and fill out the form and describe what the problem is. Once we obtain your message we will contact you back through email. You will also receive a notification in your dashboard once we reply.

What do I do if my instrument is lost or stolen?

+

If your instrument gets lost or stolen then go to our contact page and fill out the form and describe what happened to the instrument. Once received we will look at our company policies and terms of service and contact you back to see what we can do.
